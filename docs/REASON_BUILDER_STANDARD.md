# Reason Builder Standard

Purpose: make every "Why It Fits" explanation useful enough that the user can trust the recommendation without guessing.

## Required Shape

A good reason should state:

1. What the recommended card actually does.
2. What commander or deck need it addresses.
3. The specific bridge between that effect and the detected plan.
4. Any relevant constraint such as mana value, card type, token type, counter type, timing, or color identity.

## Banned Patterns

Do not use circular copy such as:

- "validated match"
- "supports the package"
- "fills the role"
- "fits the theme"
- "works well with the commander"
- "it fits because it fits"

## Accuracy Rules

- Do not claim direct commander synergy for a role fix.
- Do not claim a card protects the commander unless it protects creatures, permanents, the commander, or the key resolving window.
- Do not call any counter card proliferate support unless the card proliferates or the commander actually has a proliferate/named-counter context.
- Do not treat self-discard card selection as opponent hand pressure.
- Do not treat every instant as interaction; cantrips and card selection should be labeled as such.
- Do not present generic mana cards as mechanic synergy. For high-mana commanders, they may be described as setup only.

## Archetype-Aware Reasons

When an archetype drives a recommendation, the reason should use archetype needs:

- Late-game control finishers: explain ramp, protection, interaction, or backup engines.
- Hand-size pressure: explain opponent resource pressure or large-hand support precisely.
- Flash/reactive control: explain timing, holding up answers, or instant-speed setup.
- Voltron: explain protection, evasion, damage scaling, or tutor constraints.
- Aristocrats: explain fodder, outlet, or payoff balance.

The explanation should be specific enough to show why the card is suggested beyond sharing a word with the commander.
